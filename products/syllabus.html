The Syllabus Problem

Flight schools deserve better than Word documents and spreadsheets.

"Our syllabus is a Word doc from 2015"

CFR requirements have changed. ACS replaced PTS. But the syllabus hasn't been updated because nobody wants to spend weeks reformatting a 200-page document.

"The FSDO wants our syllabus for approval"

Part 141 schools need version-controlled, professional documentation. Exporting a properly formatted syllabus shouldn't require a graphics designer.

"I can't tell if this syllabus actually meets CFR minimums"

Manual CFR compliance checking is tedious and error-prone. When you update a lesson, how do you know the total hours still meet requirements?

"The syllabus says one thing, the schedule says another"

How It Works

From CFR requirements to trained pilot.

1. Start from a Baseline

Choose a CFR baseline (Part 61 Private, Part 141 Appendix B, etc.) or start blank. Baselines include all regulatory requirements with eCFR links.

2. Customize Your Program

Add stages, lessons, and requirements. Set equipment needs, weather minimums, and ACS standards. Compliance indicator shows real-time status.

Assign students to syllabi. They can be enrolled in multiple programs simultaneously. Progress tracks automatically as lessons complete.

Common Questions

What is a Part 141 syllabus?

A Part 141 syllabus is a detailed, FAA-approved training curriculum required for certificated flight schools. It must meet specific format and content requirements outlined in 14 CFR Part 141 and be approved by the local FSDO.

What's the difference between Part 61 and Part 141?

Part 61 allows flexible, individualized training with higher minimum hour requirements. Part 141 requires structured, FAA-approved syllabi but offers reduced hour requirements for certain certificates.

How do I create a flight training syllabus?

Start with CFR requirements for the certificate/rating. Structure lessons to meet aeronautical experience requirements. Include ground training. Align with ACS/PTS standards. AeroEdge automates this with pre-built baselines.

What are stage checks in flight training?

Stage checks are progress evaluations conducted by a different instructor to verify the student has met the objectives of a training stage before advancing. Required for Part 141, optional but recommended for Part 61.
