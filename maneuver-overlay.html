Maneuver Overlay Tool

Visualize FAA maneuvers as what they really are: time-integrated energy problems overlaid on real terrain.

Maneuvers Aren't Geometry Problems

Flight instructors explain steep turns with hand gestures. Students practice chandelles without understanding the energy they're managing. The math exists, but the visualization doesn't.

Every FAA maneuver is fundamentally an energy management exercise. You're trading altitude for airspeed, managing load factor, accounting for wind. But without seeing it, you're flying by feel alone.

The Maneuver Overlay Tool shows exactly how altitude, airspeed, and energy interact throughout each maneuver—plotted on real terrain so you can see where you'll end up relative to the airport, obstacles, and the ground.

Maneuvers Supported

From private pilot to commercial, visualize the maneuvers that matter.

Steep Turns

45° and 60° bank angles with full load factor visualization. See altitude loss, turn radius, and exactly where you'll complete the turn.

Power-Off 180

Glide management from abeam the numbers to touchdown. See how slip affects your glide path and where you'll land based on technique.

Impossible Turn Analysis

The critical GO/NO-GO decision. See exactly what altitude and conditions you need to make it back to the runway—with safety margins.

Engine-Out Glide

Where can you reach from any point? Plot glide paths on actual terrain and see your options when the engine quits.

Chandelles

The climbing 180° turn with constantly changing bank. See how energy trades between altitude and heading throughout the maneuver.

Lazy Eights

Undulating climbs and descents with reversing turns. The rhythm of energy management visualized as a continuous path.

Steep Spirals

Continuous descent in a spiral. Emergency descent technique shown with altitude loss rate and ground track.

Eights on Pylons

Figure-eight around two ground references at pivotal altitude. See the geometry and energy together.

S-Turns & Turns Around Point

Ground reference maneuvers with wind correction. See how wind affects your ground track and required bank angles.

Real Physics, Not Game-Engine Shortcuts

The same math as the EM Diagram. One source of truth.

Shared Core Engine

The Maneuver Overlay uses the same physics calculations as the EM Diagram. Turn rate, turn radius, stall speed, energy bleed—all computed from first principles, not approximations.

True Airspeed from Density Altitude

Maneuvers are computed at the actual TAS for your selected altitude and conditions. The ground track reflects what you'll actually fly, not a simplified IAS assumption.

Load Factor Effects

Higher G means higher stall speed and more drag. Energy bleed is modeled accurately throughout turns, not ignored.

Wind Convention Done Right

Enter wind the aviation way: direction it's blowing FROM. The tool converts to ground track vectors internally. Your input matches what you'd get from ATIS or a weather brief.

Terrain Awareness

Click anywhere on the map to get field elevation. Maneuvers terminate when they reach the ground. See exactly how much altitude you need to complete each maneuver.

Time Scrubber

Scrub through the maneuver second by second. See heading, bank angle, G-load, airspeed, and altitude at every point in time.
